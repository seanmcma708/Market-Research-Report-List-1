<p><ul><li>Unshielded</li><li>Shielded</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Low profile SMD power inductors are categorized into unshielded and shielded types based on their construction and magnetic shielding properties. Unshielded inductors are compact and cost-effective but can produce electromagnetic interference (EMI) due to their exposed magnetic fields. In contrast, shielded inductors feature a protective casing that minimizes EMI, making them ideal for sensitive applications despite being slightly bulkier and pricier. Both types are essential in various electronic devices, balancing size, performance, and cost according to specific design requirements.</p></p>

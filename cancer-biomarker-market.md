<p><ul><li>Protein Biomarker</li><li>Genetic Biomarker</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cancer biomarker market is primarily divided into protein biomarkers and genetic biomarkers. Protein biomarkers are molecules produced by cancer cells that can be detected in blood or tissues, providing insights into tumor presence and progression. Genetic biomarkers, on the other hand, involve mutations or alterations in DNA that indicate susceptibility to cancer or response to specific therapies. Both types are essential in personalized medicine, aiding in early detection, diagnosis, prognosis, and targeted treatment strategies for cancer patients.</p></p>
